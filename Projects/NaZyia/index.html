Is Climate Change the Greatest Threat of Our Lifetime? Or Global Warming?

Global warming refers specifically to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to rising greenhouse gas emissions. Climate change encompasses global warming but also includes a broader range of changes, such as rising sea levels, shrinking glaciers, and shifts in weather patterns. While global warming focuses on temperature rise, climate change includes the side effects of warming, like more frequent droughts and extreme weather events. These distinctions highlight that global warming is a component of the larger phenomenon of climate change.
